# Product — positioning

## What PayChecker is
**An information tool.** Workers enter or confirm facts; PayChecker computes what the award + contract say should have been paid; PayChecker surfaces the difference. Worker decides next action.

## What PayChecker is NOT
- Not legal advice.
- Not financial advice.
- Not a lawyer.
- Not an automated wage-theft accuser.
- Not a union.
- Not a complaint-filing service.

## Regulatory category
Same as the FWO Pay Calculator and the ATO tax estimator. Both are tools that compute from user-confirmed inputs and surface the result; neither asserts a fact about the user.

## Framing rules

### Words we use
- "Expected" / "received" / "difference"
- "Confirm" / "edit" / "your data"
- "We computed" / "your payslip shows" / "the difference is"

### Words we never use
- "Underpaid" / "wage theft" / "stolen" / "owed"
- "You should" / "you must" / "you need to"
- "Smart" / "AI-powered" / "intelligent"
- Any framing that makes a claim about the worker's employment.

## The Apete test
Imagine Apete shares his phone with three housemates and his employer's HR person sees his screen by accident. Nothing on the screen should feel like an accusation. Numbers and labels — yes. "You have been underpaid" — never.

## The Mia test
Imagine Mia has a manager who she suspects already retaliates against her for asking about pay. Anything in the app must be safe to be seen by that manager. The app's role is not to make the case for her; it's to give her the numbers so she can decide what to do.

## Positioning lines

- **Hero (current draft):** "Save the hours of paperwork required to check your pay."
- **Sub:** "PayChecker computes what the award and your contract say you should have received. You decide what to do next."
- **Trust note:** "Information tool, not advice. Your data stays yours."
- **Footer:** "Not legal advice. Fair Work Ombudsman: 13 13 94."

## Why this exists
Positioning is product. If the framing slips, the calculator becomes an accuser, and we are no longer in the information-tool category — at which point everything from Privacy Act exposure to user trust collapses.
